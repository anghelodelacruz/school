1 Trigonometry

2 Complex Numbers

2.1 Complex Conjugates

  • \(\overline{z_1 + z_2} = \overline{z_1} + \overline{z_2}\)
  • \(\overline{z_1 \times z_2} = \overline{z_1} \times \overline{z_2}\)
  • \(\bar{\bar{z}} = z\)

If \(z = x + yi\) (x,y not both zero), then its multiplicative inverse is denoted by \(z^{-1}\), where:

$$ z^{-1} = \frac{1}{z} = \frac{1}{x+yi} = \frac{1}{x+yi} \times \frac{x-yi}{x-yi} = \frac{x-yi}{x^2+y^2} $$

2.2 Polar Form

\begin{align} z &= r( cos(\theta) + i sin(\theta))\\ &= r cis(\theta) = r cis(\theta \pm 2n\pi), n \in J\\ \text{where } r &= \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \text{ and } tan(\theta)= \dfrac{y}{x} \end{align}
  • Cartesian Form: \(z = x + yi\)
  • \(r = \vert z \vert = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\)
  • Arg(z) = θ, -π < θ < π

2.3 Multiplication

\begin{align} z_1 z_2 &= r_1 cis(\theta_1) r_2 cis(\theta_2)\\ &= r_1 r_2 cis(\theta_1 + \theta_2) \end{align}
  • \(\vert z_1 z_2 \vert = r_1 r_2\)
  • \(arg(z_1 z_2) = \theta_1 + \theta_2\)

2.4 Division

\begin{align} \frac{z_1}{z_2} &= \frac{r_1 cis(\theta_1)}{r_2 cis(\theta_2)}\\ &= \frac{r_1}{r_2} cis(\theta_1 - \theta_2) \end{align}
  • \(\bigg\vert \dfrac{z_1}{z_2} \bigg\vert = \dfrac{r_1}{r_2}\)
  • \(arg \bigg( \dfrac{z_1}{z_2} \bigg) = \theta_1 - \theta_2\)

2.5 De Moivre's Theorem

$$ z^n = r^n cis(n\theta), n \in N $$

2.6 Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

  • Polynomial equations with real coefficients have imaginary roots which occur in pairs.

2.7 Factor Theorem

$$ P(z), P \bigg( \dfrac{b}{a} \bigg) = 0, \text{ then } \bigg( z- \dfrac{b}{a} \bigg) \text{ or } (az-b) \text{ is a factor of } P(z) $$
